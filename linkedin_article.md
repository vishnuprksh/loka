# I did a fun project recently, but its commercial aspect might be a surprise for you

### The Project: Loka
I built a simulation called Loka—an autonomous world where AI agents just live, interact, and evolve their own society without any human interference. I set up a simple environment called "The Grove" where agents have basic personality traits and two main goals: stay alive and collect gold.

### How it Works
The way it works is pretty straightforward. Each "tick" of the clock, the cycle repeats:
- Agents look around at their environment and other agents.
- They think about what happened in the past and their current needs.
- They decide whether to move, talk, or forage for resources.

### Surprising Observations
To my surprise, without any specific instructions, the agents started exhibiting complex social behaviors:
- **Negotiation**: They began arguing over the price of berries to improve their chances of survival.
- **Social Bonding**: They started befriending others just to survive longer. 
- **Work-Life Balance**: While some agents spent every second foraging, others strategically rested and negotiated later—a classic phenomenon appearing naturally.

I noticed something else that was fascinating: the dominant agent always died first. The "Mr. Careful" type was usually the one who survived the longest. I never told them to "keep some berries in hand" or "play it safe." They just acted based on their basic character traits and the situation they were in.

### The Commercial Aspect: Office Simulations
With the help of psychology, you could use an engine like this to build a simulation of a real office. 

Office politics isn't just about situational reactions; a person's "inbuilt DNA" plays a huge role. You could simulate your own office and run experiments there:
- **Predict Meeting Fatigue**: If you're planning a new daily meeting, you could predict who will actually accept it and how that decision ripples through the office talks later.
- **Identify Key Players**: You could identify who is going to play the major roles in an upcoming project if you give the system the right data.
- **Trial Experiments**: Test management changes in the simulation before trying them out on real people.

### Moving Forward
While there are definitely ethical loopholes to handle carefully, these kinds of engines could let us run plenty of social experiments before the actual experiments begin.

I'm going to increase the agent count soon to see how a larger society forms.
